    VR Exhibit on Schwarzschild Blackholes


    Royal Ontario Museum

    There is currently an unaddressed absence of VR exhibitions in museums. VR exhibits have a unique ability to engage visitors and create unique branding opportunities, as well as lasting experiences. This presents a chance to expose people to the sciences; more specifically, black holes and other phenomena that can be presented in a way that leverages existing VR technologies to make complex concepts digestible and fun. Following the success of an early few exhibits at forward-looking museums such as the National Museum of Natural History in Paris and the Smithsonian, in addition to audiences’ fascination with visuals from movies like Interstellar and Gravity, designing educational VR exhibits around astrophysics becomes an incredibly captivating idea.



    The aim of this exhibition was to showcase dazzling interactive visual displays of Schwarzschild black holes utilizing the full capabilities of existing VR technologies. To accomplish this, we worked on developing an analytical mathematical framework and parallelized shader implementations to approximate the visual phenomena of Schwarzschild black holes in real time, including aberration, beaming, Doppler shift, Lorenz contraction, and other effects. You can see a preliminary rendering we generated in the video above.

    We were invited to demo our VR exhibit on Schwarzschild Blackholes at the Royal Ontario Museum on International Astronomy Day 2019.

    Pharmacy Kiosk System


    PharmAssist+

    The motivation for this project began after learning that Ontario pharmacists will soon be allowed to diagnose and prescribe for minor ailments at their pharmacies, drastically increasing their workload. This presents a potential problem as pharmacists already have to deal with several competing forces that affect their ability to practice what they’re best at, pharmaceutical work.

    Our group wanted to come up with an innovative solution that addresses the key friction points stemming from unnecessary and time-consuming interactions between pharmacists and patients. After extensive user research and many iterations of our high-fidelity prototype, which you can take a look at here, we came up with our kiosk solution. Patients who seek to pick up a new prescription, refill an old prescription, or book a meeting with the pharmacist, can check in using the kiosk. They will then be sent a notification containing all the necessary information on when they should return to the pharmacy.

    Our group was selected to represent our class in a competition adjudicated by UX professionals from Huawei Canada, Chatham Labs, and Klick. Also, check out our teaser video above!

    “Quaranteen” Fitness Chatbot


    Personal Project

    Due to COVID-19, many young adults who are currently quarantined are struggling to acclimate to working from home. Managing productivity and physical health are becoming increasingly challenging during these uncertain times and so I wanted to build a chatbot that can help motivate quarantined youth to stay fit and healthy by giving them at-home workout exercises and personal fitness goals to aim for.

    "Quaranteen" Fitness Chatbot is a retrieval based chatbot. I compiled JSON data containing interactive language, fitness exercises and goals, to create training and testing data for the chatbot. I am currently using this data to build a 3-layer deep neural network with Keras to increase accuracy of chatbot and for the eventual chatbot interface.
