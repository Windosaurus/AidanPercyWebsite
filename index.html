    VR Exhibit on Schwarzschild Blackholes


    Royal Ontario Museum

    There is currently an unaddressed absence of VR exhibitions in museums. VR exhibits have a unique ability to engage visitors and create unique branding opportunities, as well as lasting experiences. This presents a chance to expose people to the sciences; more specifically, black holes and other phenomena that can be presented in a way that leverages existing VR technologies to make complex concepts digestible and fun. Following the success of an early few exhibits at forward-looking museums such as the National Museum of Natural History in Paris and the Smithsonian, in addition to audiences’ fascination with visuals from movies like Interstellar and Gravity, designing educational VR exhibits around astrophysics becomes an incredibly captivating idea.



    The aim of this exhibition was to showcase dazzling interactive visual displays of Schwarzschild black holes utilizing the full capabilities of existing VR technologies. To accomplish this, we worked on developing an analytical mathematical framework and parallelized shader implementations to approximate the visual phenomena of Schwarzschild black holes in real time, including aberration, beaming, Doppler shift, Lorenz contraction, and other effects. You can see a preliminary rendering we generated in the video above.

    We were invited to demo our VR exhibit on Schwarzschild Blackholes at the Royal Ontario Museum on International Astronomy Day 2019.
